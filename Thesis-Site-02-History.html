USSR Housing Renewal

History

Khrushchyovka apartments are low-cost apartment buildings that were developed in the Soviet Union during the leadership of Nikita Khrushchev. These buildings were part of giving out housing for everyone in attemps to address the severe housing shortage in the Soviet Union after World War II.

Approach Theory

“Having lost the opportunity to design individual buildings, architects invested their creative energy and talent in designing perfect masterplans. Instead of composing facades, they planned perfect compositions of buildings.”

-Placed ready-made buildings of different heights to create optimal amount of sunlight and fresh air.

-Placed trees, orchards, and ponds; to maximize the positive effect the space would have on everyday life.

-Comfortable trajectories for the residents daily routines.

-Calculated optimal distances between homes, services, and hubs of public transport, laid out most convenient paths between them.

-Designing those huge projects, the architects focused on the logic of the use of space: they proposed optimal land use intensity for each space. They tailored the sizes of different services to the size of the expected population.

“A separate khrushchevka might not be impressive, but the whole spatial system into which it was embedded is complex and extremely well thought out.”

“If we shift our focus from the architectural to the urban scale, the (current) demolition plan seems disastrous. There is no overall masterplan for the future neighborhoods.”
